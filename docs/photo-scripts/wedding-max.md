# Wedding — MAX Shot Script (~122 shots)
## "Every Moment, Every Detail"

**For the Lead Coordinator** — Read the full script the night before. This is ambitious and requires a committed Lead plus 3-5 active guest photographers working in coordination.

**Estimated Lead active time:** 6-8 hours across the day

---

## Quick Tips for All Guest Photographers

1. **Turn on Portrait Mode** for any shot of people. It blurs the background and makes everyone look amazing.
2. **Tap to focus** on faces, not backgrounds.
3. **Never use flash.** Find natural light instead.
4. **Landscape** for group shots. **Portrait** for individuals.
5. **Take 3-5 shots of everything.** Burst mode is your friend.
6. **Stay out of the aisle** during the ceremony. Shoot from your seat.
7. **Clean your lens.** Wipe it on your shirt right now.
8. **Get close.** Walk closer instead of pinching to zoom.
9. **Use the 0.5x (ultra-wide) lens** for venue shots and groups. 1x for portraits. 2x for ceremony close-ups from your seat. Never above 2x.
10. **Rule of thirds:** Place the subject's eyes at the top-third line, not dead center. Turn on the grid overlay in camera settings.
11. **Shoot through things** for artistic frames — doorways, flower arrangements, between candles, through a champagne glass.
12. **Golden hour is your superpower.** The hour before sunset produces the most beautiful, warm, soft light. Get the couple outside during this time.
13. **Anticipate, don't react.** Know what's coming next on the timeline and be in position early.
14. **Back up photos during downtime.** Upload to the shared album during cocktail hour and dinner.

## Phase 1A: GETTING READY — BRIDE/PARTNER A SIDE (2.5-3 hours before ceremony)

*Lead is on duty. 1 additional guest photographer assigned to this room.*

| # | Shot | Who | Description |
|---|------|-----|-------------|
| 1 | The Dress Hanging | LEAD | Hang on a wooden or velvet hanger against a clean background — window, exposed brick, ornate door frame. Shoot from slightly below looking up. Include shoes at the base. |
| 2 | Dress Detail Close-ups | LEAD | Lace patterns, beading, buttons down the back, the label/designer tag if meaningful. Portrait Mode, within 6 inches. |
| 3 | The Shoes | LEAD | Pair placed on a textured surface — stone step, wooden floor, velvet chair. Low angle. |
| 4 | The Flat Lay | LEAD | Arrange on a clean, light surface: invitation suite, rings in box, earrings, bracelet, perfume, shoes, veil edge, flower sprig. Shoot STRAIGHT DOWN from directly above. |
| 5 | Perfume Spritz | GUEST | Perfume bottle mid-spray with light behind it so the mist catches the light. Takes a few tries — burst mode. |
| 6 | Jewelry Close-ups | GUEST | Earrings, necklace, bracelet laid out or being put on. Focus on hands and jewelry, face in soft focus behind. |
| 7 | Hair Styling In Progress | GUEST | Stylist's hands working, bobby pins, curling iron, the mirror reflection showing the bride's face. |
| 8 | Makeup Application | GUEST | Lipstick going on, mascara wand, eyeshadow brush. Close — hands and faces. |
| 9 | Bridesmaids Getting Ready | GUEST | Robes, hair rollers, champagne glasses, laughter. Wide shot of the whole room. |
| 10 | Champagne Toast / Coffee | GUEST | Getting-ready crew clinking glasses or holding mugs. Include "bride" or "wifey" robes if they have them. |
| 11 | Stepping Into the Dress | LEAD | THE moment. Shoot from the side. Capture whoever is helping — their hands on the zipper or buttons. |
| 12 | Buttoning/Zipping the Back | LEAD | Close-up of hands doing up buttons or zipper. Slightly behind and to the side. |
| 13 | The Veil Going On | LEAD | Hands placing the veil, bride's face through the veil fabric, veil cascading down the back. |
| 14 | Bride's Full-Length Portrait | LEAD | Standing near a window, natural light. Slight angle, one foot forward. Both serious and smiling. |
| 15 | Bride Looking in Mirror | LEAD | Capture the reflection AND the real person. Stand behind and to the side. |
| 16 | Reading a Letter from Partner | LEAD | If they exchange letters. Shoot from the side, not head-on. Focus on hands holding the letter and facial expression. |
| 17 | Gift Exchange | LEAD | Opening a gift from partner. The reveal, the reaction. |
| 18 | Parent First Look | LEAD | Dad/Mom seeing their child in the dress for the first time. Position where you can see BOTH faces. Burst mode. This moment lasts 3 seconds. |
| 19 | The Bouquet | GUEST | Held naturally, lying on a surface, against the dress. Multiple angles. |
| 20 | Walking Out the Door | LEAD | Leaving the getting-ready space. The last look back. The dress gathered through the doorway. |
