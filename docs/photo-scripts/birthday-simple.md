# Birthday Party — SIMPLE Shot Script (~28 shots)

**For the Lead Coordinator** — Print this and keep it in your pocket.

---

## Quick Tips for All Photographers (Phone Users)
- Clean your lens with your shirt before shooting. Seriously. Do it now.
- Turn your phone sideways (landscape) for group shots and venue/decor. Use portrait (vertical) for individual people and tall items like cakes.
- Tap the screen on the subject's face to focus and set exposure.
- Do NOT use flash for candids — it kills the mood and the lighting. Use flash only for posed group shots in dark rooms if absolutely necessary.
- Take 3-5 shots of every moment. People blink. You will thank yourself later.
- Get close. Then get closer. Zoom with your feet, not the pinch-zoom (it degrades quality).
- For food, cake, and detail shots: shoot from above at a 45-degree angle, near a window if possible.
- Move around. The same moment looks completely different from the left side vs. the right side.

## Phase 1: PRE-PARTY SETUP (Arrive 20 min early)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Venue wide shot | Full room before guests arrive — decorations, table layout, the whole scene | LEAD |
| 2 | Cake close-up | The cake in all its glory before anyone touches it. Shoot from eye level and from above. | LEAD |
| 3 | Table setting | Food table, drink station, or snack spread — the spread looks best before people dig in | LEAD |
| 4 | Decor details | Balloons, banners, centerpieces, party favors — 2-3 close-up detail shots | LEAD |
| 5 | Gift table | The pile of wrapped presents before they are opened | LEAD |

## Phase 2: ARRIVALS & MINGLING (First 30 min)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 6 | Birthday person ready | The guest of honor before the party starts — excited, nervous, dressed up | LEAD |
| 7 | First guests arrive | Hugs, handshakes, smiles at the door — capture the energy of people showing up | LEAD |
| 8 | Guest greeting the birthday person | A warm hello, hug, or gift handoff | ANY GUEST |
| 9 | Kids playing / adults mingling | Candid shot of the first wave of socializing | ANY GUEST |
| 10 | Generational shot | The birthday person with their parents, grandparents, or kids — whoever represents family | LEAD |

## Phase 3: ACTIVITIES & GAMES (Mid-party)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 11 | Game or activity in action | Kids at a bounce house, adults playing cornhole, any activity happening | ANY GUEST |
| 12 | Laughter candid | Someone mid-laugh — the kind of photo people frame | ANY GUEST |
| 13 | Kids group candid | (Kids party) A cluster of kids doing kid things — running, playing, being chaotic | ANY GUEST |
| 14 | Adults socializing | Groups talking, catching up, drinks in hand | ANY GUEST |
| 15 | Food being enjoyed | Someone loading up a plate or mid-bite — real party energy | ANY GUEST |

## Phase 4: CAKE & CANDLES (The Main Event)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 16 | Cake being brought out | The walk from kitchen to table — capture the anticipation | LEAD |
| 17 | Singing Happy Birthday | Wide shot of the crowd singing, faces lit by candles | LEAD |
| 18 | The wish | Close-up of the birthday person's face just before blowing out candles — eyes closed, concentrating | LEAD |
| 19 | Blowing out candles | The blow itself. Take 5+ rapid shots here. This is THE photo. | LEAD |
| 20 | Reaction after candles | Cheering, clapping, the birthday person smiling — the moment right after | LEAD |
| 21 | Cake cutting / first slice | Knife in cake or the first plate being served | ANY GUEST |

## Phase 5: GIFTS (If applicable)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 22 | Opening a gift | The birthday person unwrapping — focus on their face, not the gift | LEAD |
| 23 | Reaction shot | Their expression upon seeing what is inside — surprise, delight, confusion | LEAD |
| 24 | Gift giver's face | The person who gave it watching the reaction — often the better photo | ANY GUEST |

## Phase 6: WINDING DOWN

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 25 | Big group photo | Everyone together. Have the Lead arrange this. Shoot 5 frames minimum. | LEAD |
| 26 | Birthday person with best friends | A small posed group of the inner circle | LEAD |
| 27 | Goodbye hugs | People heading out, hugging, waving — the end of the party has its own warmth | ANY GUEST |
| 28 | The aftermath | The messy table, popped balloons, empty plates — the beautiful wreckage of a good time | LEAD |
