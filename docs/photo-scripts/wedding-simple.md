# Wedding — SIMPLE Shot Script (~38 shots)
## "Capture the Day"

**For the Lead Coordinator** — Read the full script the night before. Print this and keep it in your pocket.

**Estimated Lead active time:** 3-4 hours across the day

---

## Quick Tips for All Guest Photographers

1. **Turn on Portrait Mode** for any shot of people. It blurs the background and makes everyone look amazing.
2. **Tap to focus** on faces, not backgrounds. Your phone will adjust the brightness for whatever you tap.
3. **Never use flash.** It makes everyone look washed out. Find natural light instead — windows, open doors, outdoor shade.
4. **Hold your phone sideways (landscape)** for group shots. Hold it vertically (portrait) for single-person or couple shots.
5. **Take 3-5 shots of everything.** Someone always blinks. Burst mode (hold down the shutter button) is your friend.
6. **Stay out of the aisle** during the ceremony. Shoot from your seat. The Lead will handle aisle-side angles.
7. **Clean your lens.** Seriously. Wipe it on your shirt right now. Phone lenses get smudgy and it ruins photos.
8. **Get close, then get closer.** Zoom on phones looks grainy. Walk closer instead of pinching to zoom.

## Phase 1: GETTING READY (2-3 hours before ceremony)

*Lead is on duty. 1-2 additional guest photographers helpful.*

| # | Shot | Who | Description |
|---|------|-----|-------------|
| 1 | The Dress/Suit Hanging | LEAD | Hang the outfit on a wooden hanger near a window. Step back and capture the full outfit with natural light streaming in. Include the shoes underneath if possible. |
| 2 | The Detail Spread | LEAD | Lay the rings, invitation, shoes, jewelry, perfume/cologne, and any sentimental items on a clean flat surface. Shoot straight down from above. This is called a "flat lay." |
| 3 | Hair and Makeup | ANY GUEST | Capture the bride/partner getting hair and makeup done. Shoot from the side to show the stylist working. Catch the mirror reflection if you can. |
| 4 | Getting Into the Outfit | LEAD | The moment the dress goes on, the suit jacket goes over the shoulders, the tie gets adjusted. Stay close, shoot from the side, and capture the helpers (mom zipping the dress, best man fixing the tie). |
| 5 | The Finished Look | LEAD | Full-length portrait of each partner once fully dressed. Find a window or doorway for natural light. Have them stand at a slight angle, not squared up to the camera. |
| 6 | Reaction Shot | LEAD | When the parent or partner sees the bride/groom for the first time fully dressed. Be READY — this moment is fast and emotional. Have your camera up and focused before the door opens. |
| 7 | The Wedding Party Together | ANY GUEST | Bridesmaids together, groomsmen together. Casual, laughing, toasting — not stiff and posed. |

---

## Phase 2: THE CEREMONY (30-60 minutes)

*Lead is on duty. All guest photographers shoot from their seats.*

| # | Shot | Who | Description |
|---|------|-----|-------------|
| 8 | The Venue Before Guests Arrive | LEAD | Empty ceremony space with all the decor in place. Wide shot showing the full setup — aisle, altar, flowers, chairs. |
| 9 | Guests Arriving | ANY GUEST | People finding seats, hugging each other, the energy building. Candid only. |
| 10 | The Processional | LEAD | Each person walking down the aisle — wedding party members, flower girl/ring bearer, and the bride/groom's entrance. Position yourself at the front-side of the aisle, angled so you can see faces. |
| 11 | The Partner's Reaction | LEAD | The groom/bride/partner's face the MOMENT they see their person walking toward them. This is often the most emotional photo of the day. Lock your focus on their face before the processional starts. |
| 12 | The Vows | LEAD | Both partners during vow exchange. Get both faces — the one speaking and the one listening. Shoot from the side so you can see both profiles. |
| 13 | The Ring Exchange | LEAD | Close-up of hands during the ring exchange. Move in as close as you can without being disruptive. |
| 14 | The First Kiss | LEAD | Be ready. It happens fast. Start shooting as soon as the officiant says "you may now kiss." Burst mode. |
| 15 | The Recessional | LEAD + ANY GUEST | The couple walking back down the aisle as a married couple, faces toward the guests, joyful. Guests: shoot from your seats as they pass you. |
| 16 | Guest Reactions | ANY GUEST | Tears, cheers, clapping, hugging after the ceremony. Turn your camera AWAY from the couple and toward the crowd. These are gold. |

---

## Phase 3: PORTRAITS (30-45 minutes after ceremony)

*Lead is on duty. One additional helper to wrangle family members.*

| # | Shot | Who | Description |
|---|------|-----|-------------|
| 17 | Couple Together — Wide | LEAD | Full-body shot of the couple together. Find the best-lit spot at the venue. Have them face each other at a slight angle, foreheads close. |
| 18 | Couple Together — Close | LEAD | Waist-up or face-close shot. Have them look at each other, not the camera. Catch a laugh if you can. |
| 19 | Couple with Bride's Parents | LEAD | Couple in the center, parents flanking. |
| 20 | Couple with Groom's Parents | LEAD | Same setup. |
| 21 | Couple with Both Sets of Parents | LEAD | Everyone together. |
| 22 | Couple with Siblings | LEAD | Bride's siblings, then groom's siblings, then all together. |
| 23 | Couple with Grandparents | LEAD | If grandparents are present, do this one FIRST — they may need to sit down. |
| 24 | Couple with Full Wedding Party | LEAD | Bridesmaids and groomsmen all together. One serious, one silly. |
| 25 | Couple with Their Best Friends | ANY GUEST | The ride-or-die group. Let them be natural. |

**Portrait Tips:**
- Do NOT let the sun be directly behind the group (silhouettes) or directly in their eyes (squinting). Find open shade or have the sun to the side.
- Count "3, 2, 1" before each shot so people know when to smile.
- Take at least 5 shots of every group combo. Someone always blinks.

---

## Phase 4: THE RECEPTION (3-4 hours)

*Lead is on duty for key moments. Everyone shoots candids freely between events.*

| # | Shot | Who | Description |
|---|------|-----|-------------|
| 26 | Reception Space Before Guests | LEAD | Table settings, centerpieces, dance floor, cake table, place cards, any signage. Wide shots and close-ups of details. |
| 27 | The Grand Entrance | LEAD | Couple being announced and entering the reception. Capture their faces and the crowd cheering. |
| 28 | First Dance | LEAD + ANY GUEST | Shoot from multiple angles. Lead gets the classic side view; guests shoot from their tables. |
| 29 | Parent Dances | LEAD | Father-daughter, mother-son, or any variation. Focus on faces and emotion, not feet. |
| 30 | Toasts and Speeches | LEAD | The person giving the toast AND the couple's reaction. Alternate back and forth. Guests: capture the crowd laughing or crying during speeches. |
| 31 | Cake Cutting | LEAD | The couple cutting together, the first bite/smash, their reactions. Be close and in burst mode — it happens fast. |
| 32 | The Dance Floor | ANY GUEST | Open dancing, group songs, silly moves. Get low angles, get wide shots, capture the energy. These should feel chaotic and alive. |
| 33 | Guest Candids | ANY GUEST | People laughing at tables. Kids running around. Old friends reuniting. Grandparents watching the dance floor. These are the photos people treasure most. |
| 34 | The Bouquet/Garter Toss | LEAD | The throw AND the catch. Also get the group of people reaching for it — the anticipation and chaos is the shot. |
| 35 | Couple's Quiet Moment | LEAD | If the couple steps away from the party for a breather, follow at a distance and capture them alone together. These end up being the most romantic photos of the night. |
| 36 | The Whole Room | ANY GUEST | One wide shot of the full reception in action — dance floor packed, tables buzzing, lights glowing. Stand on a chair if you need to (carefully). |
| 37 | The Send-Off | LEAD + ANY GUEST | Sparklers, bubbles, confetti, whatever is planned. Lead gets the couple running through; guests line up and shoot as they pass. |
| 38 | The Getaway | LEAD | The couple leaving. The car. The final wave. The "just married" sign. This is the closing shot of the story. |

---

## Summary

- **Lead-required shots:** 28
- **Any-guest shots:** 10
- **Total unique shots:** 38
- **Biggest tip:** The Lead should focus on the MOMENTS (vows, reactions, first kiss). Guest photographers should focus on the DETAILS and CANDIDS (table settings, guest reactions, dance floor chaos).
