# Lead Coordinator Cheat Sheet
## Print this page and keep it in your pocket.

---

## Before the Event
- [ ] Arrive 20-30 minutes early (30-45 for corporate)
- [ ] Walk the entire venue and identify your best light sources (windows, overhead fixtures)
- [ ] Shoot ALL setup/decor/detail shots before anyone arrives — this is your only chance
- [ ] Introduce yourself to the host or event organizer and confirm any must-have group shots
- [ ] Identify 2-3 reliable guests who can take "ANY GUEST" shots and brief them
- [ ] Make sure everyone has access to the shared photo album
- [ ] Charge your phone to 100% and bring a portable battery pack

## During the Event
- [ ] Do NOT stay in one spot. Move every 5-10 minutes.
- [ ] Shoot big moments from TWO positions if possible (wide, then close)
- [ ] For speeches and toasts: position at 45-degree angle to speaker so you get face AND crowd
- [ ] For cake/candle moments: get in position 2 minutes early. You cannot reshoot this.
- [ ] Text your guest photographer team 15 minutes before each major moment
- [ ] Upload photos during downtime (cocktail hour, dinner)

## Group Photo Success
- Pick a spot with **even lighting** (no half-shade, half-sun)
- Tall in back, short and kids in front
- Tell people to angle their bodies slightly (not straight-on)
- Say something unexpected before counting down — **"Everyone say MORTGAGE!"** works better than "cheese"
- Count **"3, 2, 1"** and shoot on **2** (catches people before they start moving)
- Take **5 frames minimum**
- One normal, one silly — the silly one is often better

## Photo Quality Basics
- Light source should be **BEHIND YOU**, not behind the subject
- Hold phone with **both hands**, elbows tucked to body
- For moving subjects (kids, dancing), use **burst mode** (hold shutter button)
- Get down to **kids' eye level** — do not shoot down at them
- Include context — not just faces, but hands, surroundings, what people are doing
- **Landscape** for groups and venues, **Portrait** for individuals
- **Clean your lens** every 30 minutes

## After the Event
- [ ] Collect all photos into shared album within 48 hours
- [ ] Gently nudge any guest photographers who haven't uploaded
- [ ] Final sweep: shoot the empty venue, abandoned items, melted candles — "aftermath" shots close the story

---

## Final Note to the Team

You are not professional photographers, and that is perfectly fine. Some of the most treasured photos in history were taken by friends and family — because they know the people, they know who matters, and they shoot with love, not obligation.

Your phone is more powerful than the cameras that shot most iconic photos before 2010. Trust it.

The three things that matter most: **Be in position early. Shoot more than you think you need. Focus on faces, not stuff.**

Now go make some memories.
