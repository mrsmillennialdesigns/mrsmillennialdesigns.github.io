# Anniversary — SIMPLE Shot Script (~26 shots)

**For the Lead Coordinator** — Print this and keep it in your pocket.

---

## Quick Tips for All Photographers (Phone Users)
- Clean your lens with your shirt before shooting.
- Turn your phone sideways (landscape) for group shots and venue/decor. Portrait (vertical) for individuals.
- Tap the screen on the subject's face to focus and set exposure.
- Do NOT use flash for candids. Take 3-5 shots of every moment.
- Get close. Zoom with your feet, not pinch-zoom.
- Move around — the same moment looks different from every angle.

---

## Phase 1: PRE-EVENT SETUP (Arrive 20 min early)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Venue wide shot | The full room or space — decorations, seating, ambiance | LEAD |
| 2 | Memory table / photo display | If there is a table of old photos, memorabilia, or a timeline of the couple — capture the whole thing | LEAD |
| 3 | Memory table close-ups (x2) | Two close-ups of specific items — their wedding photo, a meaningful object | LEAD |
| 4 | Cake or dessert display | The anniversary cake before it is cut | LEAD |
| 5 | Table settings / centerpieces | Flowers, candles, place cards — the details someone spent hours on | LEAD |
| 6 | Signage or custom items | A welcome sign, custom napkins, anniversary year display | LEAD |

## Phase 2: THE COUPLE & ARRIVALS

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 7 | Couple portrait — classic | The honored couple, posed, looking at the camera. Good light, clean background. Take 5 frames. | LEAD |
| 8 | Couple portrait — candid | Them looking at each other, laughing, holding hands — not posed | LEAD |
| 9 | Couple with their kids | All of their children together with them | LEAD |
| 10 | Guests arriving | People walking in, greeting each other | ANY GUEST |
| 11 | Guest greeting the couple | A warm moment — a hug, a handshake, a kiss on the cheek | LEAD |

## Phase 3: MINGLING & DINNER

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 12 | Room full of people | A wide shot once everyone has arrived — the fullness of the celebration | LEAD |
| 13 | Generational family photo | Three or four generations together — grandparents, parents, kids, grandkids | LEAD |
| 14 | Old friends reconnecting | Two people who clearly haven't seen each other in a while | ANY GUEST |
| 15 | Food being served or buffet | The dinner moment — plates being passed, buffet in action | ANY GUEST |
| 16 | Table conversation candid | A table of guests mid-conversation, laughing, eating | ANY GUEST |

## Phase 4: TOASTS & CEREMONY

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 17 | Toast-giver speaking | The person giving the toast — mid-speech, emotional, funny | LEAD |
| 18 | Couple's reaction during toast | Their faces while being toasted — tears, laughter, squeezing each other's hands | LEAD |
| 19 | Glasses raised | The room raising glasses — wide shot | ANY GUEST |
| 20 | Cake cutting | The couple cutting the anniversary cake together | LEAD |
| 21 | Feeding each other cake | If they do the classic move — be ready | LEAD |

## Phase 5: DANCING & CELEBRATION

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 22 | Couple's dance | If there is a first dance or slow dance — wide and then close | LEAD |
| 23 | Dance floor energy | Other people joining in, the crowd having fun | ANY GUEST |
| 24 | Candid laughter | The best natural smile or laugh you catch all night | ANY GUEST |

## Phase 6: GROUP PHOTOS & CLOSE

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 25 | Full group photo | Everyone at the party with the couple in the center | LEAD |
| 26 | Couple alone at end | A quiet moment — them together after the crowd thins. This is often the best photo of the night. | LEAD |
