# Birthday Party — MAX Shot Script (~56 shots)

**For the Lead Coordinator** — Print this and keep it in your pocket.

---

## Quick Tips for All Photographers (Phone Users)
- Clean your lens with your shirt before shooting. Seriously. Do it now.
- Turn your phone sideways (landscape) for group shots and venue/decor. Use portrait (vertical) for individual people and tall items like cakes.
- Tap the screen on the subject's face to focus and set exposure.
- Do NOT use flash for candids — it kills the mood and the lighting.
- Take 3-5 shots of every moment. People blink.
- Get close. Then get closer. Zoom with your feet, not the pinch-zoom.
- For food, cake, and detail shots: shoot from above at a 45-degree angle, near a window if possible.
- Move around. The same moment looks completely different from the left side vs. the right side.

## Phase 1: PRE-PARTY SETUP (Arrive 30 min early)

| # | Shot | Description | Who |
|---|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Venue exterior | The front of the house, venue, or park — establishes where the party is | LEAD |
| 2 | Venue wide interior | Full room with all decorations before guests arrive | LEAD |
| 3 | Cake hero shot | The cake from eye level with good lighting. If near a window, use that light. | LEAD |
| 4 | Cake detail | Close-up of the writing, decorations, or topper on the cake | LEAD |
| 5 | Food spread wide | The entire food table from one end | LEAD |
| 6 | Food details (x2) | Two individual dish close-ups — whatever looks best | LEAD |
| 7 | Drink station | Beverage setup — punch bowl, cooler, bar cart | LEAD |
| 8 | Balloon/banner decor | Arch, garland, banner — whatever the main decor feature is | LEAD |
| 9 | Table centerpieces | Place settings, themed plates, napkins, party hats | LEAD |
| 10 | Party favors | Goodie bags, take-home items laid out | LEAD |
| 11 | Gift table | Pile of presents — shoot from slightly above | LEAD |
| 12 | Theme details | Anything specific to the party theme — character cutouts, color scheme, custom signage | LEAD |
